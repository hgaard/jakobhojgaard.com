The mortgage industry has a branding problem. Every major lender and aggregator talks about "digital transformation" and "seamless experiences." Yet if you applied for a home loan today, you'd still be emailing PDFs, uploading payslips to clunky portals, and waiting days for someone to manually verify information that could be confirmed in seconds.

I've spent the better part of a decade inside this industry from the technology side, and the gap between the marketing and the reality is staggering.

## The real bottlenecks

The core issue isn't a lack of technology. Open banking APIs exist. Income verification services exist. Digital identity solutions exist. The problem is structural — the mortgage value chain has too many participants with misaligned incentives, and none of them individually benefit enough from fixing the whole pipeline.

Brokers want speed but don't control lender processing times. Lenders want efficiency but have compliance obligations that make automation risky. Aggregators sit in the middle, providing plumbing but rarely owning the borrower experience end-to-end.

## What a modern stack actually looks like

If you were building a mortgage platform from scratch today — which is exactly what I'm doing with Fundhaus — you'd make fundamentally different choices than the incumbents made a decade ago.

You'd start with CDR (Consumer Data Right) integration for income and expense verification. You'd use real-time property valuation APIs instead of manual appraisals for low-risk refinances. You'd build decisioning logic that can give a borrower a conditional approval in minutes, not days.

None of this is science fiction. It's just plumbing that nobody has connected properly yet.

## Why it matters

Australia has $2.1 trillion in outstanding mortgage debt. Even small efficiency gains in how that debt is originated, refinanced, and managed represent enormous value. The winners in mortgage tech won't be the ones with the flashiest app — they'll be the ones who quietly rebuild the pipes.
